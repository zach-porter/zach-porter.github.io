Video Project: Application for Grad School

This video project served as a personal statement and visual narrative for my application to graduate school. It showcases my career journey, research interests, and passion for understanding complex socio-technical systems. By combining storytelling with multimedia elements, I aimed to present a compelling case for the importance of narratives and how they impact human complex systems at multiple levels. This video reflects my commitment to using innovative formats to communicate complex ideas effectively.

Video Project: Panama, Phase Transitions, and Stories

In this video, I take viewers on a journey through three elements: a moment in time in Panama, phase transitions in both physical and social contexts, as well as the shape of stories. Through the visual medium, I draw parallels between natural phenomena and societal changes, illustrating how small shifts can lead to significant transformations. This project combines my interests in complexity science and sociology, offering a unique perspective on how we understand and interpret the world around us.
