Media projects

Video-based work that blends narrative, research interests, and sociological themes.

Video Project: Application for Grad School

This video project served as a personal statement and visual narrative for my application to graduate school. It showcases my career journey, research interests, and passion for understanding complex socio-technical systems. By combining storytelling with multimedia elements, I aimed to present a compelling case for the importance of narratives and how they impact human complex systems at multiple levels. This video reflects my commitment to using innovative formats to communicate complex ideas effectively.

Video Project: Panama, Phase Transitions, and Stories

In this video, I take viewers on a journey through three elements: a moment in time in Panama, phase transitions in both physical and social contexts, as well as the shape of stories. Through the visual medium, I draw parallels between natural phenomena and societal changes, illustrating how small shifts can lead to significant transformations. This project combines my interests in complexity science and sociology, offering a unique perspective on how we understand and interpret the world around us.

Social Simulation: Weighted Balance Opinion Model

This project utilizes Python to simulate social dynamics based on Schweighofer and Garcia’s Weighted Balance Opinion Model. This agent-based model explores how individual opinions and social influence evolve over time within a network, examining consensus and polarization dynamics.

View Simulation on GitHub

Analysis: Strength of Weak Ties Revisited

This project delves into Mark Granovetter's 'Strength of Weak Ties' concept, re-examining its relevance in modern network theory. By analyzing the role of weak ties, I explored their impact on information diffusion and community building, highlighting their continued pivotal role.

View Analysis (PDF on GitHub)

MSc Thesis: Sociological Implications of DAOs

My first Master's thesis investigates the sociological implications of Decentralized Autonomous Organizations (DAOs). Through a mixed-method case study, I analyzed how DAOs challenge traditional concepts of governance, decision-making, and social interaction, integrating insights from sociology and network science.
